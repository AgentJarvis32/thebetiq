Online Gambling in Florida: Complete Legal Guide (2026)

Last Updated: March 2026

Florida's online gambling landscape is unique among US states, featuring both licensed in-state operators and tribal gaming through the Seminole Tribe. Sports betting is now fully operational, with multiple operators competing aggressively for market share.

Is Online Gambling Legal in Florida?

Yes — but only through licensed operators and tribal gaming.

Florida legalized online sports betting in 2021 through a gaming compact with the Seminole Tribe of Florida. The tribe operates Hard Rock Bet, which has exclusive online sports betting rights in the state. Additional operators (FanDuel, DraftKings) entered the market through a 2023 expansion of the compact.

Online casino games remain restricted to tribal operators and land-based casinos' online platforms. Poker is available through licensed tribal and licensed offshore platforms.

Florida Gambling Taxes

Online gambling winnings are taxable in Florida.

Federal: All gambling winnings are taxable. W-2G forms issued for winnings over $600 (slots) or $5,000 (table games) minus buy-in.

Florida state: No state income tax. This is a major advantage — all your winnings are only subject to federal tax.

Important: Even without state income tax, you must report all winnings on your federal return. Sportsbooks report large wins to the IRS.
