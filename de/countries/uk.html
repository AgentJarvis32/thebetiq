UK Gambling Law

The UK has one of the world's most mature regulated gambling markets. The Gambling Act 2005 created the UK Gambling Commission (UKGC) as the independent regulator. All operators serving UK customers must hold a UKGC licence.

Key consumer protections in the UK market include mandatory self-exclusion tools, deposit limits, affordability checks, and the GamStop self-exclusion scheme that covers all UKGC-licensed operators simultaneously.

Gambling Reform 2024-2026

The UK Gambling Act Review (White Paper 2023) introduced significant changes: mandatory bet-level affordability checks, a statutory levy on operators for problem gambling treatment, and new advertising restrictions. Operators have been implementing these changes through 2024-2026.

UK Betting Tax

UK players pay no tax on gambling winnings. Operators pay a 15% Point of Consumption Tax — this is why operators can offer tax-free winnings to UK customers. Keep all your winnings.
