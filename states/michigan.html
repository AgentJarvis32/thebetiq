Is Online Gambling Legal in Michigan?

Yes — fully legal and regulated.

Michigan's Lawful Internet Gaming Act (passed December 2019, launched January 2021) legalized online casino games, poker, and sports betting for residents 21 and older who are physically located within state borders.

The Michigan Gaming Control Board (MGCB) oversees all licensed operators. Every licensed site is required to:

  • Verify your age and identity
  • Use geolocation to confirm you're in Michigan
  • Maintain a problem gambling program
  • Segregate player funds from operating funds

    Michigan Gambling Taxes

    Online gambling winnings are taxable income in Michigan.

    **Federal:** All gambling winnings are taxable. W-2G forms issued for winnings over $600 (lottery/slots) or $5,000 (table games/poker) minus buy-in.

    **Michigan state tax rate:** 4.25% flat income tax rate applies to gambling winnings.

    **Important:** You can deduct gambling losses up to the amount of your winnings on your federal return if you itemize. Keep records of your wins and losses.

    Operators will send a W-2G form for qualifying wins, but it's your responsibility to track and report all winnings.
