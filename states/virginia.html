Online Gambling in Virginia: Complete Legal Guide (2026)

Last Updated: March 2026

---

Virginia launched online sports betting on January 21, 2021, making it one of the earliest states to legalize the activity. A unique aspect of Virginia's market is that it's entirely online — there are no retail sportsbooks. FanDuel has dominated the Virginia market since launch. Online casinos and poker remain illegal.

Here's everything you need to know.

---

Is Online Gambling Legal in Virginia?

Partially legal — sports betting only.

Virginia's sports betting law (passed April 2020, launched January 2021) legalized online sports betting for residents 21 and older who are physically located within state borders. The Virginia Lottery oversees all licensed operators.

Virginia has a unique all-online market with no retail sportsbooks whatsoever — all betting must be done through licensed online platforms.

Online casinos and poker are NOT legal in Virginia. Only sports betting is permitted.

Every licensed sportsbook in Virginia is required to:

  • Verify your age and identity
  • Use geolocation to confirm you're in Virginia
  • Maintain a responsible gambling program
  • Segregate player funds from operating funds

    Virginia Gambling Taxes

    Sports betting winnings are taxable income in Virginia.

    **Federal:** All gambling winnings are taxable. W-2G forms issued for winnings over $600 (lottery/slots) or $5,000 (table games/poker) minus buy-in.

    **Virginia state tax rate:** 2% to 5.75% depending on income level applies to gambling winnings.

    **Important:** You can deduct gambling losses up to the amount of your winnings on your federal return if you itemize. Keep records of your wins and losses.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Do I have to be a Virginia resident to bet?

    No — you just need to be physically located in Virginia when you place your bet. Visitors and tourists can use Virginia-licensed sportsbooks.

    What's the minimum age to bet in Virginia?

    21+, no exceptions. All sportsbooks verify your age before allowing real money betting.

    Why is Virginia all-online with no retail sportsbooks?

    Virginia chose to authorize only online sports betting, not retail locations. This makes it unique among sports betting states.

    What's the #1 sportsbook in Virginia?

    FanDuel dominates Virginia's market with over 50% of total wagering volume. It was the first operator to launch and has maintained its lead since January 2021.

    Can I use one account for multiple sportsbooks?

    Yes. Each operator maintains separate accounts. You can have accounts with FanDuel, DraftKings, BetMGM, and all other licensed sportsbooks in Virginia simultaneously.

    When will online casinos be legal in Virginia?

    There is currently no bill pending to legalize online casinos in Virginia. The state has only authorized sports betting at this time.
