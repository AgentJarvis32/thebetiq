Online Gambling in Ohio: Complete Legal Guide (2026)

Last Updated: March 2026

---

Ohio launched online sports betting on January 1, 2023, making it one of the most recent states to legalize the activity. The market has grown rapidly with six major operators now licensed, and Ohio's competitive landscape rivals established states like Michigan and New Jersey. However, online casinos and poker remain illegal.

Here's everything you need to know.

---

Is Online Gambling Legal in Ohio?

Partially legal — sports betting only.

Ohio's sports betting law (passed December 2021, launched January 2023) legalized online sports betting for residents 21 and older who are physically located within state borders. The Ohio Casino Control Commission oversees all licensed operators.

Online casinos and poker are NOT legal in Ohio. Only sports betting is permitted.

Every licensed sportsbook in Ohio is required to:

  • Verify your age and identity
  • Use geolocation to confirm you're in Ohio
  • Maintain a responsible gambling program
  • Segregate player funds from operating funds

    Online casinos are NOT legal in Ohio. Only sports betting is permitted.

    Ohio Online Poker

    Online poker is NOT legal in Ohio. Players must use retail poker rooms or travel to states where it's legal (Michigan, Pennsylvania, New Jersey, Nevada).

    Ohio Gambling Taxes

    Sports betting winnings are taxable income in Ohio.

    **Federal:** All gambling winnings are taxable. W-2G forms issued for winnings over $600 (lottery/slots) or $5,000 (table games/poker) minus buy-in.

    **Ohio state tax rate:** 3.75% income tax rate applies to gambling winnings.

    **Important:** You can deduct gambling losses up to the amount of your winnings on your federal return if you itemize. Keep records of your wins and losses.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Do I have to be an Ohio resident to bet?

    No — you just need to be physically located in Ohio when you place your bet. Visitors and tourists can use Ohio-licensed sportsbooks.

    What's the minimum age to bet in Ohio?

    21+, no exceptions. All sportsbooks verify your age before allowing real money betting.

    Can I use one account for multiple sportsbooks?

    Yes. Each operator maintains separate accounts. You can have accounts with DraftKings, FanDuel, BetMGM, and all other licensed sportsbooks in Ohio simultaneously.

    When will online casinos be legal in Ohio?

    There is currently no bill pending to legalize online casinos in Ohio. The state has only authorized sports betting at this time.

    Can I use offshore sportsbooks in Ohio?

    Yes, but there's no state protection if something goes wrong. With six excellent licensed options available in Ohio, there's little reason to use offshore sites here.
