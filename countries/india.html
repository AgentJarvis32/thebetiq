Betting Laws in India

India's gambling laws are a patchwork of colonial-era legislation and state-specific rules. The Public Gambling Act of 1867 is the central law — but it predates the internet by 130 years and doesn't explicitly address online betting.

Key points: No federal law prohibits Indian players from using offshore betting sites. The Information Technology Act doesn't specifically block gambling sites. State laws vary significantly — Goa, Sikkim, and some other states have licensed forms of gambling.

In practice: tens of millions of Indians use offshore betting sites regularly. No Indian bettor has been prosecuted for placing bets on an offshore site.
