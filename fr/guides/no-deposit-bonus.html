How No Deposit Bonuses Actually Work

Free money sounds simple. The fine print makes it complicated. Here's what to know:

Wagering Requirements

The most important number. A $25 bonus with 20x wagering means you must bet $500 total before withdrawing any winnings.

**At $5/spin on slots:** 500 hands to clear. Realistic in one session.

**At $10/hand blackjack (if allowed):** 50 hands. Easy.

Always check which games count toward the wagering requirement. Slots usually count 100%. Table games often count 10-25% or not at all.

Maximum Withdrawal Limits

No deposit bonuses almost always cap what you can win from the free credit. Common caps:

  • $50-$100 at licensed US books
  • $100-$500 at offshore books (higher limits are more common)

    • You can win more than the cap playing, but only the capped amount is withdrawable. Don't expect to turn $25 into $10,000.

    Game Restrictions

    Read the terms carefully. Some no deposit bonuses are locked to specific games — often one or two featured slots. Others exclude high-RTP games like blackjack and video poker entirely.

    Time Limits

    Most no deposit bonuses expire in 24-72 hours if not used, and wagering must be completed within 7-30 days. Don't claim the bonus unless you're ready to play.

Their first deposit bonus (often worth $500-$1,000+) may no longer be available. If you plan to be a regular player, consider whether the no deposit offer or the first deposit bonus is more valuable.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Can I keep what I win from a no deposit bonus?

    Yes, up to the maximum withdrawal limit specified in the terms. Winnings above the cap are forfeited.

    Do no deposit bonuses require a credit card?

    You'll need to verify your identity (name, address, date of birth, last 4 of SSN) but typically don't need to enter payment info to claim a no deposit bonus.

    Can I claim no deposit bonuses at multiple casinos?

    Yes — each casino is separate. You can claim one at BetMGM and one at Caesars simultaneously if you're in a state where both operate.

    Why do casinos offer no deposit bonuses?

    Player acquisition. A new player who signs up and tries the product with free credits has a high conversion rate to real money depositor. The casino considers the bonus cost a marketing expense.

    What's the catch with no deposit bonuses?

    Wagering requirements are the main catch. Maximum withdrawal limits are the second catch. Read the terms before playing.
