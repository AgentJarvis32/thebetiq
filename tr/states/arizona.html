Online Gambling in Arizona: Complete Legal Guide (2026)

Last Updated: March 2026

---

Arizona launched online sports betting in September 2021, with unique tribal partnerships that give Native American nations a significant role in the market. The state has become a competitive landscape with five major licensed operators, though online casinos and poker remain unavailable.

Here's everything you need to know.

---

Is Online Gambling Legal in Arizona?

Partially legal — sports betting only.

Arizona's sports betting law (passed May 2021, launched September 2021) legalized online sports betting for residents 21 and older who are physically located within state borders. The Arizona Department of Gaming oversees licensed operators.

A unique aspect of Arizona's framework is strong tribal nation involvement — several operators partner with or operate under tribal gaming agreements.

Online casinos and poker are NOT legal in Arizona. Only sports betting is permitted.

Every licensed sportsbook in Arizona is required to:

  • Verify your age and identity
  • Use geolocation to confirm you're in Arizona
  • Maintain a responsible gambling program
  • Segregate player funds from operating funds

    Arizona Gambling Taxes

    Sports betting winnings are taxable income in Arizona.

    **Federal:** All gambling winnings are taxable. W-2G forms issued for winnings over $600 (lottery/slots) or $5,000 (table games/poker) minus buy-in.

    **Arizona state tax rate:** 2.55% to 4.5% depending on income level applies to gambling winnings.

    **Important:** You can deduct gambling losses up to the amount of your winnings on your federal return if you itemize. Keep records of your wins and losses.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Do I have to be an Arizona resident to bet?

    No — you just need to be physically located in Arizona when you place your bet. Visitors and tourists can use Arizona-licensed sportsbooks.

    What's the minimum age to bet in Arizona?

    21+, no exceptions. All sportsbooks verify your age before allowing real money betting.

    Can I use one account for multiple sportsbooks?

    Yes. Each operator maintains separate accounts. You can have accounts with DraftKings, FanDuel, BetMGM, and all other licensed sportsbooks in Arizona simultaneously.

    What's unique about Arizona's sports betting market?

    Arizona has significant tribal involvement. Several operators partner with or operate under tribal gaming agreements, giving Native American nations a stake in the market's success.

    When will online casinos be legal in Arizona?

    There is currently no bill pending to legalize online casinos in Arizona. The state has only authorized sports betting at this time.
