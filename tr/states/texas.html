Is Online Gambling Legal in Texas?

Online casino games and poker: No licensed options exist.

Texas has no licensed online casino gambling and no online poker. The state legislature has been debating sports betting legislation for years without passing anything.

Sports betting: Not yet legal, though bills keep getting introduced.

    Texas Gambling Laws: The Fine Print

    **Texas Penal Code Section 47.02** makes gambling illegal — but with an important carve-out: private places among friends are excluded. The law targets commercial gambling operations, not individual players.

    **Federal law:** The Unlawful Internet Gambling Enforcement Act (UIGEA) makes it illegal for banks to process transactions to unlicensed gambling sites. It does NOT make it illegal for you as a player to place bets. Offshore books get around this via crypto and alternative payment processors.

    **In plain English:** No Texas player has ever been prosecuted for betting on an offshore site. The legal risk to individual players is essentially theoretical.

    Texas Gambling Tax Rules

    Even without licensed gambling, winnings from offshore sites are taxable income at the federal level. Texas has no state income tax, which is one advantage.

    **Federal:** Report all gambling winnings on your 1040. Offshore sites won't send you a W-2G (they're not required to), but the IRS still expects you to report the income.

    Daily Fantasy Sports in Texas

    DFS is fully legal in Texas under the Texas Skill Games Act. DraftKings and FanDuel both operate openly with full marketing in the state.

    **How it works:** You pick players, they earn points based on real-game stats, you win based on your lineup's performance vs. other entries. It's skill-based (legally), not gambling.

    This is the only licensed "sports betting adjacent" product available in Texas right now.
