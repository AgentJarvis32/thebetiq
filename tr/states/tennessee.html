Online Gambling in Tennessee: Complete Legal Guide (2026)

Last Updated: March 2026

---

Tennessee launched online sports betting on November 1, 2020, making it the first state to legalize mobile sports betting (before even launching retail). Tennessee's market is unique for its all-online structure and the 10% hold requirement, which is the highest edge requirement for sportsbooks in any US state. Online casinos and poker are not legal.

Here's everything you need to know.

---

Is Online Gambling Legal in Tennessee?

Partially legal — sports betting only.

Tennessee's sports betting law (passed May 2019, launched November 2020) legalized online sports betting for residents 21 and older who are physically located within state borders. The Tennessee Gambling Commission oversees all licensed operators.

Tennessee is unique for being entirely online with no retail sportsbooks. Additionally, Tennessee has a 10% hold requirement — the highest edge requirement for sportsbooks in the US — meaning books must hold at least 10% of all wagers.

Online casinos and poker are NOT legal in Tennessee. Only sports betting is permitted.

Every licensed sportsbook in Tennessee is required to:

  • Verify your age and identity
  • Use geolocation to confirm you're in Tennessee
  • Maintain a responsible gambling program
  • Segregate player funds from operating funds
  • Comply with the 10% hold requirement

    Understanding Tennessee's 10% Hold Requirement

    What is it? Tennessee requires sportsbooks to hold at least 10% of all wagers. This is the highest hold requirement in the US (most states allow 4-6%).

    What does this mean for bettors? The 10% hold means Tennessee sportsbooks have higher built-in profit margins than operators in other states. While you'll still find competitive odds on major sports, the hold requirement slightly reduces expected value for bettors on all markets.

    Example: In other states, a -110 moneyline might be available. In Tennessee, books are more likely to hold -115 or worse on equivalent markets due to the 10% hold requirement.

    Tennessee Gambling Taxes

    Sports betting winnings are taxable income in Tennessee.

    **Federal:** All gambling winnings are taxable. W-2G forms issued for winnings over $600 (lottery/slots) or $5,000 (table games/poker) minus buy-in.

    **Tennessee state tax rate:** Tennessee has NO state income tax on wages, but gambling winnings are subject to federal tax only.

    **Important:** You can deduct gambling losses up to the amount of your winnings on your federal return if you itemize. Keep records of your wins and losses.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Do I have to be a Tennessee resident to bet?

    No — you just need to be physically located in Tennessee when you place your bet. Visitors and tourists can use Tennessee-licensed sportsbooks.

    What's the minimum age to bet in Tennessee?

    21+, no exceptions. All sportsbooks verify your age before allowing real money betting.

    Why does Tennessee have a 10% hold requirement?

    Tennessee's legislature specifically mandated the 10% hold as a regulatory requirement for all sportsbooks. The reason was to ensure state tax revenue from the market.

    Does the 10% hold mean odds are worse in Tennessee?

    Not necessarily worse overall, but built-in margins are higher. The hold affects all books equally, so they compete on other factors like sign-up bonuses, loyalty programs, and live betting features rather than purely on odds.

    Is Tennessee all-online like Virginia?

    Yes — Tennessee is entirely online with no retail sportsbooks, similar to Virginia. All betting must be done through licensed mobile apps or websites.

    Can I use one account for multiple sportsbooks?

    Yes. Each operator maintains separate accounts. You can have accounts with FanDuel, DraftKings, BetMGM, and all other licensed sportsbooks in Tennessee simultaneously.
