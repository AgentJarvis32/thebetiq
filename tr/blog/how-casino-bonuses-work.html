How Casino Bonuses Work: Wagering Requirements Explained

April 2, 2026 · 13 min read ·
Guides

Casino bonuses sound amazing on the surface: "Get $200 free!" But that $200 isn't actually yours until you jump through a series of hoops that most players don't fully understand. The biggest hoop is the wagering requirement — a confusing mechanic that determines whether a bonus is actually valuable or a waste of your time.

In this comprehensive guide, we decode exactly how casino bonuses work, break down wagering requirements in plain English, explain every hidden term you need to know, and show you how to evaluate whether a bonus is worth claiming. By the end, you'll understand bonuses better than 95% of casino players.

Types of Casino Bonuses

Online casinos use different bonus structures depending on your status as a player. Here are the main categories:

Welcome / Sign-Up Bonus

Your first bonus as a new player. Usually split across your first 2–4 deposits. Example: 100% match on your first deposit up to $200, meaning if you deposit $200, you get $200 free (total balance: $400).

Deposit Match Bonus

The casino matches a percentage of your deposit. A "100% match up to $100" means: deposit $100, receive $100 bonus (total: $200). A "50% match up to $150" means: deposit $200, receive $100 bonus (total: $300).

Free Spins Bonus

You receive a number of free spins on a specific slot game. Winnings from those spins are credited as bonus funds subject to wagering requirements before withdrawal.

Reload Bonus

A bonus for existing players on subsequent deposits. Usually less generous than welcome bonuses but more flexible.

Loyalty / VIP Bonus

Rewards for regular play. Accumulated through your wagers or casino points. Higher-value players get better terms: lower wagering requirements, higher max cashouts, faster verification.

No-Deposit Bonus

Free cash or spins with zero deposit required. Extremely rare now and heavily restricted, but available from aggressive casinos trying to build player base.

Wagering Requirements Explained: The #1 Confusion Point

A wagering requirement (also called "playthrough" or "rollover") is the amount you must bet before bonus money becomes real, withdrawable cash. This is where 90% of bonus confusion happens.

The Simple Formula

Total Wagering Needed = Bonus Amount × Wagering Multiplier

If you claim a $50 bonus with a 35x wagering requirement:

$50 × 35 = $1,750

You need to place bets totaling $1,750 before the bonus balance and any winnings from it become withdrawable. This doesn't mean you spend $1,750 — it means the sum of all your bets reaches $1,750.

Real Example:
You claim a $100 deposit match bonus (100% up to $100) by depositing $100. Your balance: $200.
Wagering requirement: 35x
Wagering needed: $100 (bonus) × 35 = $3,500 total bets

You play slots at $2 per spin. You need 1,750 spins before the bonus clears. After 1,750 spins, your remaining balance becomes real cash withdrawable.

Why Do Casinos Have Wagering Requirements?

To prevent abuse. Without wagering requirements, players would deposit $100, get $100 free, and immediately withdraw $200. Casinos would go broke. The wagering requirement forces you to play, giving the casino a chance to win back the bonus through house edge before you can cash out.

What's a "Fair" Wagering Requirement?

The industry standard is 30–50x. Here's what to expect:

  • 15–25x: Generous (rare). Favors the player significantly.
  • 30–40x: Standard. Most welcome bonuses fall here. Achievable with smart play.
  • 50x+: Punishing. The odds are heavily against clearing this profitably.
  • 70x+: Trap. Designed to look free but mathematically impossible to beat.
⚠️ Critical Rule: Don't claim a bonus with wagering over 50x. The house edge on slots means you'll lose more than the bonus is worth before you finish wagering. It's mathematically unfavorable.

How Wagering Is Calculated: The Meter Mystery

When you play, your casino account shows a "wagering progress meter." Here's what it actually tracks:

Contribution System

Not all bets count equally toward wagering. This is the hidden gotcha most players miss. Each game has a "contribution rate" to wagering:

Game Type Contribution Rate Example
Slots 100% $10 bet counts as $10 toward wagering
Video Poker 50% $10 bet counts as $5 toward wagering
Blackjack 10% $10 bet counts as $1 toward wagering
Roulette 5% $10 bet counts as $0.50 toward wagering
Live Dealer 0% $10 bet counts as $0 toward wagering

This is why game choice matters enormously. If your bonus requires $3,500 in wagering and you play blackjack (10% contribution), you actually need to place $35,000 in bets. If you play slots (100% contribution), you need $3,500.

Same Bonus, Different Games = Different Reality

Bonus: $100 with 35x wagering = $3,500 wagering needed

Playing Slots: $3,500 in bets to clear (100% contribution)
Playing Blackjack: $35,000 in bets to clear (10% contribution)

The blackjack route is 10x harder because table games intentionally count less toward wagering.

Tracking Your Progress

Most casinos show a progress bar or percentage in your account dashboard. It updates in real-time as you play. Always check this after each session to know how much wagering remains.

Game Contribution Rates: Why This Matters

Contribution rates are the secret weapon of bonus strategy. Here's the full breakdown by game category:

Slots: 100% Contribution

Every dollar bet on slots counts toward wagering. This is your best option for clearing bonuses. Choose high-RTP, low-volatility slots and bet conservatively to maximize the number of rounds.

Video Poker: 25–50% Contribution

Varies by casino and variant. Some casinos contribute 50%, others 25%. Check before playing. Video poker has some of the best RTPs in the casino if you play optimal strategy, but low contribution rates make it impractical for bonus clearing.

Blackjack: 5–10% Contribution

Deliberately restricted. Blackjack has one of the lowest house edges when played optimally (as low as 0.5%), so casinos only let it count 5–10% toward wagering. Not recommended for clearing bonuses.

Roulette: 2–5% Contribution

Even more restricted than blackjack. House edge is 2.7% (European) or 5.26% (American), but the contribution rate is so low that you'd need to bet $70,000+ to clear a modest $1,000 wagering requirement. Avoid.

Baccarat: 10–25% Contribution

Varies. Better than roulette, but still impractical. The low contribution rate is punishment for baccarat's favorable odds.

Live Dealer: 0% Contribution

Usually excluded entirely. Your bonus funds can't be used on live dealer games, or if they can, they contribute 0% to wagering. This is intentional — casinos don't want high-value players clearing bonuses on live dealer tables.

Sports Betting / Other: 0% Contribution

Bonuses can't be used outside the casino vertical. Your casino bonus won't transfer to the sportsbook.

Bonus Restrictions & Limits

Beyond wagering requirements, bonuses come with additional restrictions that limit how much you can actually win:

Maximum Cashout Limit

The largest amount you can withdraw from bonus winnings. A $100 bonus with a max cashout of $200 means even if you turn that bonus into $10,000 in winnings, you can only withdraw $200. This is brutal but common, especially on no-deposit and small deposit bonuses.

Always check max cashout before claiming. If it's too low relative to the bonus size, it's not worth your time.

Time Limit / Expiry

Most bonuses expire in 7–30 days. If you don't complete wagering before expiry, the bonus and any winnings from it are forfeited. Some casinos are stricter: your bonus expires if you're inactive for 3 days.

Time Limit Trap:
You claim a bonus that expires in 7 days. You need $3,500 in wagering. That's 1,750 spins at $2 each. If you play 3 hours per day at 10 spins per minute, you need at least 2–3 days of play. But if you can only play 1 hour per day, you won't finish. Bonuses with longer expiries (30 days) are more forgiving.

Minimum Bet Requirement

Many bonuses come with a minimum bet size required while clearing the bonus. Typical: "Bets under $5 void the bonus." This prevents players from grinding the bonus with tiny bets indefinitely.

Country Restrictions

Bonuses may not be available to players from certain countries. Always verify your location is eligible before registering, otherwise you claim a bonus you can never withdraw.

Payment Method Restrictions

A bonus claimed with a credit card deposit may not be available if you later deposit via crypto or e-wallet. Read the terms to ensure your payment method qualifies.

Bonus Strategy: Evaluating a Bonus

Here's the framework to evaluate whether a casino bonus is worth claiming:

Step 1: Check the Wagering Requirement

If it's 50x or higher, skip it. The math doesn't work in your favor.

Step 2: Calculate the Expected Loss

Average slot RTP is 96–97%. House edge is 3–4%.

If wagering = $3,500, expected loss = $3,500 × 3% = $105.

For the bonus to be valuable, it should be larger than the expected loss. A $50 bonus with $3,500 wagering = $105 expected loss. You're underwater before you start. A $200 bonus makes more sense.

Step 3: Check Maximum Cashout

If max cashout is less than the bonus amount, that's a red flag. A $100 bonus with a $75 max cashout means you lose value immediately.

Step 4: Verify Game Eligibility

Confirm that slots contribute 100% to wagering and that you can play the games you enjoy. If you prefer table games and only they count 10%, don't claim the bonus.

Step 5: Check Expiry Time

30-day expiries are comfortable. 7-day expiries are tight. If you can't carve out 5–10 hours of play time in that window, skip it.

Casino Bonus Terms You Must Know

Sticky Bonus

A bonus that you must wager before you can withdraw, but which is "sticky" — meaning you can't withdraw it directly even after wagering. Only net winnings from the bonus become real money. This is deliberately unfavorable to players.

Non-Sticky Bonus

After you complete wagering, the remaining bonus balance itself is withdrawable. Much better than sticky.

Cashable Bonus

After wagering, both the bonus and winnings are withdrawable. The best type (rare).

Non-Cashable Bonus

The bonus itself is never withdrawable. Only net winnings count. Standard for most casinos.

Bonus Abuse Prevention

If a casino suspects you're deliberately gaming their bonus system (e.g., claiming big bonuses, immediately withdrawing when you lose), they may flag your account, confiscate winnings, or close you. Play naturally and you won't trigger this.

KYC (Know Your Customer) Verification

Before withdrawing, casinos require identity verification. You'll need to provide ID, proof of address, and sometimes proof of payment method. This takes 24 hours to several days. Be prepared and don't expect instant withdrawals.
